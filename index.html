🔬 What Did the AI Analyze?

The AI performed a multi-stage behavioral analysis on your uploaded video. Here are the 6 behavioral categories that were evaluated:

A — Eye Contact & Facial Interaction

Gaze direction, face orientation, eye contact avoidance, and facial emotion variation across frames.

B — Social Interaction

Engagement with nearby people, response to interaction attempts, imitation of actions, and proximity avoidance.

C — Repetitive Behaviors

Hand flapping, body rocking, spinning, finger flicking, repetitive jumping, and repetitive clapping (motion cycle analysis).

D — Motor Pattern Differences

Toe walking, stiff movement, unusual posture, joint angles, and walking gait analysis.

E — Attention Patterns

Long fixation on objects, watching spinning objects, difficulty shifting gaze, and gaze fixation duration.

F — Sensory Response Behaviors

Covering ears, sudden agitation, turning away from stimuli, hand-to-head gestures, and motion bursts.

📐 How the Risk Score is Calculated

The final Autism Behavior Risk Score is a weighted combination of the category scores:

Risk Score =
  0.25 × Eye Contact Score
+ 0.30 × Repetitive Behavior Score
+ 0.20 × Social Interaction Score
+ 0.15 × Attention Pattern Score
+ 0.10 × Sensory Response Score

High Risk: Score ≥ 0.70  |  Moderate Risk: Score 0.40–0.69  |  Low Risk: Score < 0.40

Video Recording Guidelines

  • 📹 Ensure good lighting - face should be clearly visible
  • 👤 Face the camera directly for best eye contact analysis
  • ⏱️ Record for 30 seconds to 2 minutes
  • 🎯 Keep your face centered in the frame
  • 🔇 Record in a quiet environment
  • 🚫 Avoid wearing sunglasses or items that cover your face

Note: Video analysis provides preliminary observations only. For accurate diagnosis, please consult with a healthcare professional.
