About Autism Spectrum Prediction

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a diverse set of conditions characterized by unique patterns of social interaction, communication, and focused interests. Our application uses scientifically backed clinical guidelines and machine learning to analyze these behavioral and cognitive patterns to help identify potential neurodivergent traits.

What We Look For

🧠

Cognitive Processing

We analyze performance in detail-focused tasks (like visual pattern search). Exceptionally fast local processing can be a strong indicator of neurodivergent perception.

⚡

Executive Function

Through Go/No-Go inhibition games, we measure impulse control and sustained attention. High rates of false alarms help identify challenges associated with ASD and ADHD.

👥

Social Cognition

We track reaction times and accuracy in emotion recognition. Delays or inaccuracies in rapid facial affect recognition are key diagnostic markers.

🎥

Behavioral Tracking

Our AI video framework tracks eye contact, repetitive motions, sensory responses, and focal attention to calculate a comprehensive pediatric risk score.

Our Prediction Guidelines

The predictions generated by this platform are based on heuristic models and ML classifiers trained on established clinical criteria (such as the DSM-5). The algorithms assign targeted "risk weights" to different inputs to calculate the probability of an autism profile:

  • Questionnaire Data: Assesses historical developmental milestones, behavioral history, and social difficulties.
  • Facial & Posture Algorithms: Weights reduced eye contact (25%), repetitive motor movements (30%), and lack of social smiling (20%) highest among visual indicators using MediaPipe tracking.
  • Algorithmic Heuristics: Replicates the Embedded Figures Test and standard inhibition tasks, comparing reaction latencies and error rates against neurotypical baselines.

This is a screening tool only and does not provide medical diagnosis. Please consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation.
